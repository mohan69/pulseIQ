## 1. Demo Objective

Demonstrate how PulseIQ converts enterprise operating complexity into an AI-native intelligence layer — showing leaders what is happening, what is broken, what AI can improve, and what measurable business outcomes can be achieved.

**Success metric:** Customer agrees to a 2-week paid pilot conversation.

| Role | What they care about |
|------|---------------------|
| CEO / Managing Director | Business growth, competitive advantage, ROI |

## 4. Talk Track by Screen

### Opening (0:00 – 3:00)

**Say this:**

> "Thank you for your time today. I want to show you something that takes your operating complexity — the systems, the processes, the handoffs, the manual work — and turns it into a clear AI transformation roadmap.
>
> PulseIQ is not another dashboard. It maps your current operating model, identifies where AI can create measurable business impact, and gives your team a 90-day execution plan.
>
> I will walk you through a real example using a manufacturing company similar to yours. This will take about 30 minutes."

---

### Landing Page (3:00 – 5:00)

**Say this:**

> "This is PulseIQ. The core promise is simple:
>
> In 2 weeks, we map your current operating model, identify AI transformation opportunities, estimate business impact, and give your CXO team a 90-day execution roadmap.
>
> The platform is designed for enterprises like yours — manufacturing, EPC, financial services, or talent-heavy operations — where there is significant manual work, disconnected systems, and no real-time visibility."

**Point to:**
- Headline and tagline
- The two CTAs: Start Assessment, View Demo
- The "Built For" section showing industry verticals

---

### Dashboard (5:00 – 8:00)

**Say this:**

> "This is the assessment dashboard. You can see we have four demo assessments loaded — these represent real enterprise scenarios.
>
> Let me show you Bharat Heavy Fabrications — a ₹500Cr manufacturing company with 1,200 employees. Notice the transformation score: 32 out of 100. That tells you immediately where this company stands.
>
> You can see the opportunity value — ₹22-33Cr — and there are 4 quick wins identified. This is the kind of visibility you get before we even start the deep analysis."

**Point to:**
- Company name, industry, revenue, employee count
- Transformation score (color-coded)
- Opportunity value card
- Quick wins count
- Pain point badges

**Ask the customer:**

> "Does this profile look similar to what you see in your organization?"

---

### Enterprise Intake (8:00 – 12:00)

**Say this:**

> "Now let me show you what we capture in the intake. This is the company profile — systems landscape, pain points, and strategic priorities.
>
> Look at the systems: SAP ERP, legacy MES, Excel for quality tracking, Tally for finance. This is typical for Indian manufacturing companies.
>
> Here are the pain points — production scheduling delays, manual material reconciliation, no real-time OEE visibility. These are not hypothetical. These are the exact problems we hear from operations leaders every week.
>
> And here are the strategic priorities — 85% OEE, 20% waste reduction, real-time CXO visibility. These are the outcomes the business wants."

**Point to:**
- Current systems grid (ERP, CRM, HRMS, Finance, etc.)
- Pain points list with numbering
- Strategic priorities list

**Ask the customer:**

> "Which of these pain points resonate most with your current situation?"

---

### Operating Model Mapper (12:00 – 16:00)

**Say this:**

> "This is where we map the operating model. We look at each department, the processes, the systems used, handoffs, and manual work percentage.
>
> Take Production — 450 people, two core processes. Production Scheduling is 70% manual. Quality Inspection is 85% manual. That is a huge productivity drain.
>
> Look at the handoffs — Planning to Shop Floor, Shop Floor to Quality. Every handoff is a potential failure point. Every manual step is a potential error.
>
> This mapping is what makes the AI analysis possible. Without understanding the current state, you cannot identify where AI creates value."

**Point to:**
- Department cards with headcount
- Process cards with manual work percentage badges
- Systems used tags
- Handoffs and risks
- Reporting pain points

**Ask the customer:**

> "If you had to estimate, what percentage of your operations would you say is still manual?"

---

### AI Transformation Opportunities (16:00 – 21:00)

**Say this:**

> "Now this is the core of PulseIQ. These are AI-generated transformation opportunities, ranked by business impact and implementation complexity.
>
> Look at the first one — AI-Powered Production Scheduling. It is a Quick Win. The impact is ₹8-12Cr annually through a 40% reduction in scheduling cycle time. The current process takes 2 days. AI can do it in 2 hours.
>
> Here is Computer Vision Quality Inspection — this is Strategic. It will detect defects with 99.5% accuracy versus the current 85% manual detection. The impact is ₹4-6Cr annually.
>
> And Predictive Material Reconciliation — another Quick Win. Eliminates ₹2-3Cr of annual waste through automated tracking.
>
> Notice the total opportunity value — ₹22-33Cr. These are not made-up numbers. They are derived from your actual pain points, your manual work percentages, and industry benchmarks."

**Point to:**
- Total opportunity value card
- Quick wins count
- Individual opportunity cards with priority badges
- Impact type and estimated impact
- Recommended owner
- "Why it matters" section

**Ask the customer:**

> "Which of these opportunities would have the biggest impact on your business right now?"

---

### Executive Cockpit (21:00 – 25:00)

**Say this:**

> "This is the Executive Cockpit. It gives you a single view of where you stand and what to do next.
>
> The Transformation Score is 32 out of 100. That means there is significant room for improvement — and that is good news, because it means the opportunity is large.
>
> Top 5 bottlenecks — these are the things holding the business back. Production scheduling delays, quality data lag, material waste, reactive maintenance, delayed finance reporting.
>
> Top 5 executive actions — these are the decisions the leadership team needs to make. Approve the AI scheduling pilot. Commission IoT sensors. Mandate digital quality tracking.
>
> AI Adoption Readiness — Medium. Strong IT infrastructure, but needs change management. This tells you exactly what to invest in."

**Point to:**
- Transformation Score gauge
- Opportunity value
- Quick wins and high complexity counts
- Top 5 bottlenecks list
- Top 5 executive actions list
- AI adoption readiness indicator

**Ask the customer:**

> "If you had to pick one bottleneck to eliminate this quarter, which would it be?"

---

### Future Operating Model (25:00 – 27:00)

**Say this:**

>
> Before: Manual scheduling, Excel-based tracking, reactive quality control, 48-hour data lag.
>
> After: AI-optimized scheduling, real-time production tracking, computer vision quality, instant data visibility.
>
> We also recommend specific AI agents, workflow automations, and integration points. This is not vague — it is a concrete blueprint for transformation."

**Point to:**
- Before/after comparison cards
- Recommended AI agents
- Workflow automations
- Governance recommendations

---

### Roadmap (27:00 – 29:00)

**Say this:**

>
> 30 days — Discovery and Quick Wins. Complete the production data audit, deploy AI scheduling pilot on Line 1, implement real-time OEE dashboard.
>

- Three phase cards
- Individual milestones with owners and KPIs
- Pilot scope recommendation

## 6. Questions to Ask the Customer

### During the demo:

1. "Does this profile look similar to what you see in your organization?"
2. "Which of these pain points resonate most with your current situation?"
3. "If you had to estimate, what percentage of your operations would you say is still manual?"
4. "Which of these opportunities would have the biggest impact on your business right now?"
5. "If you had to pick one bottleneck to eliminate this quarter, which would it be?"

### At the pilot close:

6. "Who else from your leadership team should be involved in the scoping call?"
7. "What does your current AI adoption roadmap look like?"
8. "Is there a specific business outcome you are targeting for this quarter?"
9. "What would success look like for you in 90 days?"
10. "Shall we schedule the scoping call for this week?"
